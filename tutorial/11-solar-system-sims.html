1. Solar System 3D Gravitator - by far the simplest
http://arnholm.org/astro/software/ssg/index.htm
sudo dpkg -i solsysgrav...
increase the days step to 30 and watch the planets fly off their orbit into space!!  Find out what speed you need to send the earth off!  :)


The following may not be suitable for netbooks or low-speed cpu's/graphics cards.  I can use Celestia and Stellarium on my Lenovo S200 OK, but they stagger a bit with low cpu capacity on the Acer Aspire One.

2. Openuniverse 
http://www.openuniverse.org/
download the tar.gz file, unpack it and install (after checking required dep's) with ./configure, make, make install
depends GL library (Mesa is recommended http://www.mesa3d.org/,,,), 
GLUT (or not if get Mesa demo) freeglut3 and freeglut3-dev are in Debian repos.
GLUI http://glui.sourceforge.net/... also libglui2c2 and libglui-dev are in Debian repos, but I'm not sure yet if they are what's needed.
I needed to install flex as well
sudo apt-get install flex xutils-dev libglui2c2 libglui-dev
(installs: freeglut3 freeglut3-dev libegl1-mesa libegl1-mesa-drivers libgl1-mesa-dev libgl1-mesa-glx libglapi-mesa libglu1-mesa libglu1-mesa-dev libxt-dev
mesa-common-dev)

3. Stellarium 
http://www.stellarium.org/
sudo apt-get install stellarium
installs stellarium and stellarium-data 

You can stand on any place on earth or on any planet and zoom in on other planets and stars, even satellites.  Just click on an object and press / to zoom in.  Press \ to zoom back out.  Great for learning where all the constellations are.
user guide http://stellarium.org/wiki/...


4. Celestia-GTK 
http://pat.suwalski.net/celestia/
sudo apt-get install celestia
installs celestia celestia-common celestia-gnome libgtkglext1

With this one you can select any star or solar system object and go to it, but things will look better with the addons installed.  If you get lost in space you can return to Sol and look for close objects (i.e. our planets) while zooming in/out a little.  Click on any object and hit G to go there (that will definitely help you get lost in space if you just keep going out and out!)
Go to earth with "H3G" and then press L a few times till you see time speeded up and earth rotating (K will slow down time and J will reverse time).  Press Shift-] and [ to bring on the earth's shadow and Shift-Left/Right to move around the earth, Shift-Up/Down to adjust position on the axis and Y to sync to orbit.  Press F to cancel sync orbit.  Press I to turn off cloud.  Press and hold End to move out from Earth, and Home to come back -or G if you've gone a long way out.
Press HFC to center on the Sun and P to turn on planet names, O to toggle orbit lines, and speed up things to watch the planets orbiting the sun.  Shift-Up to make their orbits more circular.  Press any number to focus on a planet (G to go to it).
Cursor keys adjust your orientation -as does drag with the mouse.  Press Enter to type a target, any first letter and Tab to select from a list, then Enter again and G.
