Many laptops are hampered by a maximum volume that seems much too quiet and it's particularly annoying when you're trying to play some media that was encoded with poor audio settings, so that there's almost nothing heard at all. In such cases, it would be nice to amplify the sound above the normal 100% full volume, but it doesn't go (well we I'm talking laptop speakers, not plugin ones).

VLC can boost to about 125% and pulse audio has some settings to boost to 150%. But can it be done on a system with just Alsa installed? It sure can!

First we need to create an Alsa config file, and in there we will make a new control that makes the default audio channel its slave, and then itself sets a higher max dB limit.

There are quite a few forum posts (like here) with ideas, but when I tried following them I had endless problems with Alsa not able to recognise the slave.

I then found this config layout which is simpler and it worked for my box, in that I boosted my audio, but it left my system unstable - suspend and shutdown commands froze the desktop. So its not yet perfect.
